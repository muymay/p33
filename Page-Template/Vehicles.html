Your Vehicles

Whole life insurance covers for an individual’s lifetime and offers cash accumulation, such as funds for retirement or education. This insurance plan has higher returns than a Municipal Bond portfolio. A municipal bond portfolio is where an investor purchases a municipal bond, which is loaning money in exchange for interest payments.
Term insurance provides coverage for a certain period of time at a low-cost. The insurance protects against financial hardships that may occur after the death of an individual, which can be beneficial when you’re trying to pay off a debt or doing business deals.
Equities are the shares that you purchase from a company. A bond is a loan issued by the government where you can get interest payments in return. Real assets are physical assets, such as real estate.
Term life insurance provides financial protection for a fixed amount of years if an unfortunate event occurs. The financial protection helps your entire family with their savings and expenses. Term insurance is also affordable and flexible since an individual can adjust their coverage for benefits.
Bonds are where investors loan their money for a fixed interest rate and they would receive their investment back at the end of the term. Bonds also have a lower market risk compared to stocks. Tax-exempt bonds include municipal bonds, or bonds issued by the government. Taxable bonds include corporate bonds.
